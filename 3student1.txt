
Part One
CHAPTER I

 SCARLETT O'HARA was not beautiful, but men seldom realized it when caught by her charm as
the Tarleton twins were. In her face were too sharply blended the delicate features of her
mother , a Coast aristocrat of French descent , and the heavy ones of her florid Irish
father. But it was an arresting face , pointed of chin , square of jaw. Her eyes were pale
green without a touch of hazel , starred with bristly black lashes and slightly tilted at the ends. Above them , her thick black brows slanted
upward , cutting a startling oblique line in her magnolia-white skin
